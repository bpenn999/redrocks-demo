Part D Prescription Drug Plans

Stand-alone drug coverage that keeps your prescriptions affordable — now with a $2,100 yearly out-of-pocket cap.

Part D plans are run by private insurers and each has its own formulary — the list of drugs it covers and the tier (and price) of each. Two people on the same medications can pay wildly different amounts depending on which plan they pick, which is why we compare plans against your specific prescriptions rather than guessing.

As of 2026, once your out-of-pocket drug spending hits $2,100 for the year, you pay $0 for covered drugs the rest of the year. That cap is a major change, and we make sure your plan and pharmacy choices take full advantage of it.

If you go without creditable drug coverage after your Initial Enrollment Period, Medicare adds a permanent late-enrollment penalty to your premium — so enrolling on time, even in a low-cost plan, protects you long-term.

What's included

  • Covers prescription drugs Original Medicare doesn't
  • New $2,100 annual out-of-pocket cap (2026) thanks to the Inflation Reduction Act
  • We match your exact drug list to the lowest-cost plan formulary
  • Avoids the Part D late-enrollment penalty that lasts as long as you have Part D
  • Optional Medicare Prescription Payment Plan spreads costs over the year

Who it's for

Anyone on Original Medicare or a Medigap plan who takes prescriptions, or who wants to avoid the lifelong late-enrollment penalty.
