ACA Health Insurance (Under 65)

Affordable Care Act Marketplace plans for Utahns who aren't yet on Medicare — with premium tax credits that lower what you pay each month.

If you're under 65 and don't have coverage through an employer, the Affordable Care Act Marketplace is where you'll find quality individual and family health plans. Many Utahns qualify for premium tax credits that lower the monthly cost — often by a lot — based on household income and size.

Plans are grouped into metal tiers: Bronze (lower premium, higher out-of-pocket), Silver (the sweet spot for anyone who qualifies for cost-sharing reductions), and Gold (higher premium, lower out-of-pocket). The right tier depends on how much care you expect to use and your budget. Enroll during Open Enrollment (Nov 1 – Jan 15) or a Special Enrollment Period after a qualifying life event.

We help early retirees and self-employed clients pick the right Marketplace plan now — and then plan the handoff to Medicare when they turn 65 so there's no gap and no penalty. For your full range of Marketplace options, you can also visit HealthCare.gov directly.

What's included

  • Individual & family plans through the Health Insurance Marketplace (HealthCare.gov)
  • Premium tax credits (subsidies) can dramatically lower your monthly premium
  • Metal tiers — Bronze, Silver, Gold — to match your budget and expected use
  • Cost-sharing reductions on Silver plans for those who qualify
  • A smooth bridge to Medicare when you turn 65

Who it's for

For individuals and families under 65 — early retirees, self-employed, and anyone without employer coverage who needs quality health insurance.
