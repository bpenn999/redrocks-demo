Medicare Advantage (Part C)

All-in-one plans that bundle hospital, medical, and usually drug coverage — often with $0 premiums and extras like dental, vision, and hearing.

Who it's for: Great for people who want one card, predictable copays, and built-in extras — and who are comfortable using a plan's provider network.

Medicare Supplement (Medigap)

Standardized plans that pay the gaps in Original Medicare so you can see any doctor that accepts Medicare, nationwide, with very predictable costs.

Who it's for: Ideal if you want maximum freedom to choose doctors, travel often, or prefer predictable bills over the lowest premium.

Full details →
💊

Part D Prescription Drug Plans

Stand-alone drug coverage that keeps your prescriptions affordable — now with a $2,100 yearly out-of-pocket cap.

Who it's for: Anyone on Original Medicare or a Medigap plan who takes prescriptions, or who wants to avoid the lifelong late-enrollment penalty.

ACA Health Insurance (Under 65)

Affordable Care Act Marketplace plans for Utahns who aren't yet on Medicare — with premium tax credits that lower what you pay each month.

Who it's for: For individuals and families under 65 — early retirees, self-employed, and anyone without employer coverage who needs quality health insurance.
