Medicare Supplement (Medigap)

Standardized plans that pay the gaps in Original Medicare so you can see any doctor that accepts Medicare, nationwide, with very predictable costs.

Medicare Supplement (Medigap) policies are standardized by the federal government, so a Plan G from one carrier covers exactly the same gaps as a Plan G from another — the difference is price and company service. That makes shopping straightforward once you know which plan letter fits you.

The tradeoff versus Medicare Advantage is simple: a higher monthly premium in exchange for the freedom to see any doctor who accepts Medicare, nationwide, with little or no cost at the point of care. For people who travel, split time between states, or want to avoid network surprises, that peace of mind is worth it.

Timing matters. During your one-time 6-month Medigap Open Enrollment Period (starting the month you're 65 and enrolled in Part B), carriers cannot deny you or charge more for health conditions. We help you use that window well.

What's included

  • Works alongside Original Medicare — no networks, any Medicare provider nationwide
  • Plan G and Plan N are the most popular for people new to Medicare
  • Very predictable out-of-pocket costs year to year
  • Pairs with a stand-alone Part D drug plan
  • Best pricing usually locks in during your 6-month Medigap Open Enrollment

Who it's for

Ideal if you want maximum freedom to choose doctors, travel often, or prefer predictable bills over the lowest premium.
