The Building Blocks

The Coverage We Compare

Medicare has a few moving parts. Here's the plain-English version of what we'll help you weigh.

Parts A & B

Original Medicare

The federal foundation — hospital (Part A) and medical (Part B) coverage. Most people start here, then add to it.

Part C

Medicare Advantage

An all-in-one alternative from private carriers that often bundles drug, dental, vision, and hearing — usually with a network.

Medigap

Medicare Supplement

Fills the gaps in Original Medicare so your out-of-pocket costs are predictable, and lets you keep any doctor that accepts Medicare.

Part D

Prescription Drug Plans

Standalone drug coverage that pairs with Original Medicare or a Supplement. We check your specific medications against each plan.

Questions

Good to Know

Is there any cost to use this? +
No. Our help is completely free to you. Licensed agents are compensated by the insurance carriers, so you get personalized guidance at no cost and with no obligation to enroll.
When should I enroll in Medicare? +
Most people have a 7-month Initial Enrollment Period around their 65th birthday — the 3 months before, your birthday month, and the 3 months after. If you're still working with employer coverage, different rules may apply. The quiz helps us flag your window so you don't miss it.
Do I have to switch my current coverage? +
Not at all. Many people take the quiz just to understand their options or make sure their current plan still fits. There's never any pressure to change anything.
Will you keep my information private? +
Yes. Your information is only used to prepare your plan comparison and have a licensed agent follow up. We never sell your data.
What if I still have questions after the quiz? +
That's exactly what the free review is for. A licensed agent walks through your matches in plain English, answers everything, and only helps you enroll if and when you're ready.
