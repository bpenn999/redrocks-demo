When can you enroll in Medicare? Every enrollment window, decoded

IEP, AEP, OEP, and Special Enrollment Periods — what each one is, the exact dates, and which one applies to you right now.

By the Everything Senior Insurance Data Desk · Reviewed by Christian Brindle, Founder & Licensed Insurance Advisor · Updated 2026

Your Initial Enrollment Period (IEP)

Your first chance to enroll is a 7-month window around your 65th birthday: the 3 months before your birthday month, your birthday month, and the 3 months after. Enrolling in the three months before your birthday means coverage can start the month you turn 65.

  • Starts 3 months before your 65th birthday month
  • Includes your birthday month
  • Ends 3 months after — 7 months total

Annual Enrollment Period (AEP): Oct 15 – Dec 7

Every fall, anyone on Medicare can switch Medicare Advantage or Part D plans for the following year. This is the time to review your plan against next year's premiums, drug formularies, and provider networks — changes we catch during your annual review.

Medicare Advantage Open Enrollment (Jan 1 – Mar 31)

If you're already in a Medicare Advantage plan, you get one chance early in the year to switch to a different Advantage plan or return to Original Medicare (with a Part D plan).

Special Enrollment Periods (SEPs)

Life events — moving, losing employer coverage, qualifying for Extra Help — can open a Special Enrollment Period outside the normal windows. If your situation changed, you may be able to enroll now; ask us to check.
