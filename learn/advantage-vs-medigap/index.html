Medicare Advantage vs. Medigap: how to actually decide

The real tradeoffs between an all-in-one Advantage plan and Original Medicare + a supplement — with a simple framework to choose.

By the Everything Senior Insurance Data Desk · Reviewed by Christian Brindle, Founder & Licensed Insurance Advisor · Updated 2026

Two different philosophies

Medicare Advantage trades a low (often $0) premium and built-in extras for using a provider network and variable copays. Medigap trades a higher monthly premium for the freedom to see any Medicare provider nationwide with very predictable costs. Neither is 'better' — they suit different lives.

Choose Medicare Advantage if…

  • You want the lowest premium and extras like dental, vision, and hearing
  • Your doctors are in the plan's network
  • You mostly get care locally
  • You're comfortable with copays that vary by service

Choose Medigap + Part D if…

  • You want to see any doctor that accepts Medicare, anywhere in the U.S.
  • You travel or split time between states
  • You prefer predictable bills over the lowest premium
  • You want to avoid network and referral hassles

The timing catch most people miss

Medigap's best pricing and guaranteed acceptance usually require enrolling during your 6-month Medigap Open Enrollment. Wait too long and carriers can medically underwrite you. If you're leaning Medigap, timing can matter more than the plan letter.
