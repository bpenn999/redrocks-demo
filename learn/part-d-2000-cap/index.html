The new $2,100 Part D drug cap: what it means for you

Starting in 2026, your out-of-pocket prescription costs are capped at $2,100 a year. Here's how the cap works and how to use it.

By the Everything Senior Insurance Data Desk · Reviewed by Christian Brindle, Founder & Licensed Insurance Advisor · Updated 2026

What changed in 2026

Thanks to the Inflation Reduction Act, Medicare Part D now caps your annual out-of-pocket drug spending at $2,100. Once you hit that limit, you pay $0 for covered drugs for the rest of the year. The old 'donut hole' coverage gap is gone.

The Medicare Prescription Payment Plan

You can also choose to spread your out-of-pocket drug costs across the year in monthly installments instead of paying a big bill at the pharmacy counter. This is optional and free to opt into — useful if your costs are front-loaded early in the year.

How we help

We compare your exact medication list against each plan's formulary and preferred pharmacies to find the lowest total cost — not just the lowest premium. The cap protects you, but plan choice still decides how quickly and cheaply you get there.
