Medicare in Salt Lake City & Utah: a local guide

What Medicare looks like on the Wasatch Front — plan availability, local networks, and how to get unbiased help nearby.

By the Everything Senior Insurance Data Desk · Reviewed by Christian Brindle, Founder & Licensed Insurance Advisor · Updated 2026

A strong plan market on the Wasatch Front

Salt Lake County beneficiaries have access to a robust Medicare Advantage market — 41 Medicare Advantage plans for CY2026 (24 MA-PD plus 17 Special Needs Plans), 17 of the 24 MA-PD plans at a $0 premium, according to the CMS Medicare Plan Landscape — plus 10 stand-alone Part D plans in the Idaho/Utah region and the full range of Medigap options. Wide choice is great news, and also why comparing carefully matters.

Local networks and hospitals

Advantage plan networks in Utah are often built around major systems like Intermountain Health and University of Utah Health. Which plan is right depends on where you already get care — we verify your specific doctors and facilities are in-network before you enroll.

Getting unbiased local help

As an independent, Salt Lake City–based advisor, Christian Brindle works with many carriers rather than one — so the recommendation is based on your needs, not a single company's lineup. Comparing your options costs you nothing.
