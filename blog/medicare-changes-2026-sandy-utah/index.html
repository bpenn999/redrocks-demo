Medicare in Sandy, Utah for 2026: Every Change That Matters and What to Do About It

The 2026 Part D cap rises to $2,100, Part B hits $202.90, and Salt Lake County's plan lineup shifts. What Sandy seniors should do before fall enrollment.

By the Everything Senior Insurance Data Desk · Reviewed by Christian Brindle, Founder & Licensed Insurance Advisor · Published July 10, 2026 · Updated July 11, 2026

Key takeaways

  • The Part D out-of-pocket cap rises to $2,100 for 2026 (it was $2,000 in 2025) — after that, covered drugs cost you $0 for the rest of the year.
  • The standard Part B premium is $202.90/month in 2026, with a $283 annual deductible; the Part A hospital deductible is $1,736 per benefit period.
  • Salt Lake County's 2026 lineup: 41 Medicare Advantage plans, 17 of 24 MA-PD plans at $0 premium, top rating 4.5 stars.
  • The standard maximum Part D deductible rises to $615 in 2026 — many plans charge less, and formularies shift every year.
  • Your move: review your plan during AEP (October 15 – December 7) against next year's actual numbers, not this year's assumptions.

The headline change: a $2,100 cap on your drug costs

The biggest 2026 number for Sandy and Salt Lake City seniors is the Medicare Part D out-of-pocket cap: once your spending on covered prescription drugs reaches $2,100 for the year, you pay $0 for covered drugs through December 31. The cap — created by the Inflation Reduction Act — replaced the old 'donut hole' coverage gap entirely, and it adjusts annually (it was $2,000 in 2025).

For anyone on expensive brand-name drugs, insulin, or a long medication list, this is real protection: your worst-case pharmacy year now has a hard ceiling. But note what the cap doesn't do — it doesn't standardize how fast you reach it. Plans still differ on deductibles, tiers, and preferred pharmacies, so the months before you hit $2,100 can cost very different amounts depending on which plan you chose.

Spread the cost: the Medicare Prescription Payment Plan

Alongside the cap, every Part D plan must offer the Medicare Prescription Payment Plan — an option that spreads your out-of-pocket drug costs across the calendar year in monthly installments instead of charging you at the pharmacy counter. It's free to opt into, it doesn't reduce what you owe, and it's most useful for people whose drug costs are front-loaded — say, an expensive fill every month starting in January.

If January and February pharmacy bills have ever forced hard choices, ask your plan (or us) about enrolling in the payment option for 2026. It converts a $600 January into roughly equal monthly payments you can budget around.

What Original Medicare costs in 2026

Most people get Part A (hospital insurance) premium-free because they paid Medicare taxes during their working years — but Part A still carries a $1,736 deductible per benefit period in 2026. A benefit period isn't a calendar year: it starts when you're admitted and ends after 60 days without inpatient care, so it's possible to pay it more than once in a year.

Part B (medical insurance) has a standard premium of $202.90 per month in 2026 with a $283 annual deductible. Higher earners pay more through IRMAA — an income-based surcharge Social Security calculates from your tax return two years back, so 2026 surcharges are based on 2024 income. If your income has dropped since then because of retirement or another life change, you can ask Social Security to use your current income instead by filing Form SSA-44.

  • Part D standard maximum deductible: $615 (2026)
  • Part B standard premium: $202.90/mo (2026)
  • Part B annual deductible: $283 (2026)
  • Part A hospital deductible: $1,736 per benefit period (2026)

Salt Lake County's 2026 plan lineup

Every fall, carriers reset premiums, formularies, provider networks, and extra benefits for the coming year — and the county lineup itself shifts as plans enter and exit. For 2026, Salt Lake County offers 41 Medicare Advantage plans (24 standard MA-PD plus 17 Special Needs Plans), with 17 of the 24 MA-PD plans at a $0 premium and a top overall rating of 4.5 stars, per the CMS Medicare Plan Landscape.

Around the Wasatch Front the numbers vary more than people expect: Utah County has 21 MA-PD plans (14 at $0), Davis County has 24 (17 at $0), and Weber County has 19 (13 at $0) for 2026. If you've moved — even one county over — your plan menu changed with you, and a Special Enrollment Period likely opened.

The change nobody announces: your own plan's fine print

The quiet risk each year isn't a new law — it's your current plan changing underneath you. Every September, plans mail an Annual Notice of Change (ANOC) listing next year's premium, deductible, copays, formulary moves, and network changes. Most people never read it. Then January arrives and a medication that cost $10 is suddenly on a higher tier, or a specialist quietly left the network.

Read the ANOC — or hand it to someone whose job is reading them. A ten-minute review in October routinely saves hundreds of dollars and a lot of frustration the following year. This is exactly what our free annual review is for: we check your actual doctors and prescriptions against your plan's actual 2026 terms, and only suggest a switch if it genuinely helps you.

Your 2026 action checklist

Here's what we'd tell a neighbor in Sandy to do this fall, in order:

  • Mark the Annual Enrollment Period: October 15 – December 7 (changes take effect January 1, 2026)
  • Read your plan's Annual Notice of Change when it arrives in September
  • Write down every prescription, dose, and your preferred pharmacy
  • List your doctors and preferred hospital (Intermountain, University of Utah Health, or others)
  • Compare your current plan against the 2026 lineup — or have an independent advisor run it free
  • If your income dropped since 2024, ask about Form SSA-44 to reduce an IRMAA surcharge
  • Ask about the Medicare Prescription Payment Plan if drug costs bunch up early in your year

The bottom line for Sandy seniors

2026 is a genuinely better year to be a Medicare beneficiary than most: the $2,100 drug cap is the strongest cost protection Part D has ever had, and Salt Lake County's plan market is deep and competitive. The premiums tick up — that's real — but the bigger financial swing for most households is plan fit, not the standard premium.

Don't auto-renew blind. A short review against next year's real numbers is the cheapest insurance there is.

Frequently asked questions

What is the Medicare Part D out-of-pocket cap for 2026?

$2,100. Once your out-of-pocket spending on covered Part D drugs reaches $2,100 in 2026, you pay nothing for covered drugs the rest of the year.

How much is the Part B premium in 2026?

The standard premium is $202.90 per month with a $283 annual deductible. Higher-income beneficiaries pay an IRMAA surcharge based on their 2024 tax return.

When can I change my Medicare plan for 2026?

The Annual Enrollment Period runs October 15 – December 7, with changes effective January 1. Those already in a Medicare Advantage plan get one more switch during the January 1 – March 31 Open Enrollment Period.

Did my Salt Lake County plan change for 2026?

Almost certainly something changed — premiums, drug tiers, network, or extras. Your plan's Annual Notice of Change (mailed in September) lists everything; review it or have an advisor do it free.

What is the Medicare Prescription Payment Plan?

A no-cost option every Part D plan must offer that spreads your out-of-pocket drug costs into monthly installments across the year instead of paying at the pharmacy counter.
