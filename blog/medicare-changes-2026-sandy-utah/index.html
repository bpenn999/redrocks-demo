Medicare in Sandy, Utah for 2026: What's Changing and What It Means for You

The 2026 Part D out-of-pocket cap rises to $2,100, Part B premiums tick up, and plan lineups shift. Here's what Sandy and Salt Lake City seniors should know before fall enrollment.

By the Everything Senior Insurance Data Desk · Reviewed by Christian Brindle, Founder & Licensed Insurance Advisor · Published July 10, 2026

Key takeaways

  • The Part D out-of-pocket cap rises to $2,100 for 2026 (up from $2,000 in 2025).
  • The standard Part B premium is $202.90/mo in 2026, with a $283 annual deductible.
  • The Part A hospital deductible is $1,736 per benefit period in 2026.
  • Plans change every year — review yours during the Annual Enrollment Period, October 15 – December 7.

The headline change: a $2,100 Part D drug cap

The biggest 2026 change for Sandy and Salt Lake City seniors is the Medicare Part D out-of-pocket cap. Thanks to the Inflation Reduction Act, once your out-of-pocket spending on covered prescription drugs reaches $2,100 in 2026, you pay $0 for covered drugs the rest of the year. That's up modestly from the $2,000 cap in 2025, and the old 'donut hole' coverage gap is gone. If your drug costs are front-loaded early in the year, you can also spread them over 12 months through the Medicare Prescription Payment Plan.

What Part A and Part B cost in 2026

Most people get premium-free Part A because they paid Medicare taxes while working, but Part A still has a hospital deductible of $1,736 per benefit period in 2026. Part B has a standard monthly premium of $202.90 and a $283 annual deductible in 2026; higher earners pay more through IRMAA, which is based on your income from two years prior (2024 for 2026).

  • Part D out-of-pocket cap: $2,100 (2026)
  • Part B standard premium: $202.90/mo (2026)
  • Part B annual deductible: $283 (2026)
  • Part A hospital deductible: $1,736 per benefit period (2026)

Local plan lineups shift too

Every fall, carriers change premiums, drug formularies, provider networks, and extra benefits for the coming year. For 2026, Salt Lake County has 41 Medicare Advantage plans (24 MA-PD plus 17 Special Needs Plans), with 17 of the 24 MA-PD plans at a $0 premium. A benefit that was great this year can quietly change next year — which is exactly why an annual review matters.

What Sandy & SLC seniors should do now

Mark the Annual Enrollment Period: October 15 through December 7. That's your window to switch Medicare Advantage or Part D plans for 2026. Before then, list your current doctors and medications so you (or your advisor) can check them against next year's plans. If you're already enrolled, don't auto-renew blindly — a five-minute review can catch a formulary change that would cost you hundreds.

Frequently asked questions

What is the Medicare Part D out-of-pocket cap for 2026?

$2,100. Once your out-of-pocket spending on covered Part D drugs reaches $2,100 in 2026, you pay $0 for covered drugs for the rest of the year.

How much is the Part B premium in 2026?

The standard Part B premium is $202.90 per month in 2026, with a $283 annual deductible. Higher-income beneficiaries pay more through IRMAA.

When can I change my Medicare plan for 2026?

During the Annual Enrollment Period, October 15 through December 7. A Medicare Advantage Open Enrollment Period also runs January 1 – March 31 for those already in an Advantage plan.
