Choosing a Medicare Advantage Plan in Salt Lake County (2026 Guide)

Salt Lake County has 41 Medicare Advantage plans for 2026 — 17 of the 24 MA-PD plans at $0 premium. Here's how to pick the right one for your doctors and prescriptions, from a Sandy-based advisor.

By the Everything Senior Insurance Data Desk · Reviewed by Christian Brindle, Founder & Licensed Insurance Advisor · Published July 11, 2026

Key takeaways

  • Salt Lake County offers 41 Medicare Advantage plans for CY2026 (24 MA-PD + 17 Special Needs Plans).
  • 17 of the 24 MA-PD plans have a $0 monthly premium — but you still pay your Part B premium ($202.90 in 2026).
  • The top plan in the county carries a 4.5-star CMS rating; out-of-pocket maximums range from $3,500 to $9,250.
  • The right plan depends on whether your Sandy/SLC doctors are in-network and your drugs are covered — not the premium alone.

How many Medicare Advantage plans are in Salt Lake County for 2026?

For the 2026 plan year, Salt Lake County residents can choose from 41 Medicare Advantage plans, according to the CMS Medicare Plan Landscape: 24 standard Medicare Advantage Prescription Drug (MA-PD) plans plus 17 Special Needs Plans (SNPs) for people with specific conditions or dual Medicare/Medicaid eligibility. That's a lot of choice for one county — which is good news, and also why comparing carefully matters.

What will a plan cost in Sandy and Salt Lake City?

Of the 24 MA-PD plans, 17 carry a $0 monthly plan premium for 2026. A $0-premium plan does not mean free care: you still pay your Medicare Part B premium ($202.90 standard in 2026), and you'll have copays and coinsurance as you use services. What protects you is the plan's in-network maximum out-of-pocket (MOOP) limit, which for Salt Lake County 2026 plans ranges from $3,500 to $9,250. Once you hit that ceiling, the plan pays 100% of covered in-network care for the rest of the year.

  • 17 of 24 MA-PD plans: $0 plan premium (2026)
  • Part B premium still applies: $202.90/mo standard (2026)
  • In-network out-of-pocket max: $3,500–$9,250 (2026)
  • Top overall CMS star rating in the county: 4.5 stars

Does the plan cover your doctors and hospitals?

This is the question that actually decides which plan is right. Medicare Advantage plans in Utah are usually built around a network — often anchored by Intermountain Health or University of Utah Health. Before you enroll, we confirm that your primary care doctor, any specialists, and your preferred hospital (say, Alta View or Intermountain Medical Center for many Sandy-area residents) are in the plan's network. A $0-premium plan is only a good deal if it covers the care you actually use.

Do your prescriptions fit the plan's drug list?

Every MA-PD plan has its own formulary — the list of drugs it covers and the tier (and cost) of each. Two people on the same medications can pay very different amounts depending on the plan. We match your exact prescription list to each plan's formulary and preferred pharmacies. In 2026, your Part D out-of-pocket drug spending is capped at $2,100 for the year — a major protection — but plan choice still decides how quickly and cheaply you get there.

A simple way to choose

List your doctors, your prescriptions, and the pharmacy you like. Then compare plans against that exact list — networks first, drugs second, extras (dental, vision, hearing) third, and premium last. As an independent, Sandy-based agency near 9000 South, we do this comparison with you at no cost, and we review it again each fall so your plan keeps fitting as it changes.

Frequently asked questions

How many Medicare Advantage plans are available in Salt Lake County in 2026?

41 Medicare Advantage plans — 24 MA-PD plans and 17 Special Needs Plans — per the CMS Medicare Plan Landscape for CY2026.

Are there $0-premium Medicare Advantage plans in Sandy, Utah?

Yes. 17 of the 24 Salt Lake County MA-PD plans have a $0 monthly plan premium for 2026. You still pay your Part B premium and any copays.

Does a $0-premium plan mean free healthcare?
