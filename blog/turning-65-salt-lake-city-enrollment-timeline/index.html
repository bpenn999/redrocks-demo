Turning 65 in Salt Lake City? Your 2026 Medicare Enrollment Timeline

A clear, month-by-month Medicare enrollment timeline for people turning 65 in Salt Lake City and Sandy — when your window opens, what to do, and how to avoid lifelong penalties.

By the Everything Senior Insurance Data Desk · Reviewed by Christian Brindle, Founder & Licensed Insurance Advisor · Published July 9, 2026

Key takeaways

  • Your Initial Enrollment Period (IEP) is a 7-month window around your 65th birthday.
  • Enroll in the 3 months before your birthday month so coverage can start the month you turn 65.
  • Miss it without other creditable coverage and you can face lifelong Part B and Part D penalties.
  • A local, independent advisor can map your options in about 20 minutes at no cost.

When does your Medicare window open?

If you're turning 65 in Salt Lake City or Sandy, your first chance to enroll is your Initial Enrollment Period (IEP) — a 7-month window that starts 3 months before your birthday month, includes your birthday month, and ends 3 months after. Enrolling in those first three months means your coverage can begin the month you turn 65, with no gap.

  • 3 months before your birthday month — enroll here for coverage on time
  • Your birthday month
  • 3 months after — 7 months total

Still working past 65?

If you (or your spouse) are still working and covered by an employer group health plan, you may be able to delay Part B without penalty and enroll later through a Special Enrollment Period. But the rules depend on employer size and how your coverage is structured — get it checked before you delay, because a wrong assumption here can trigger a lifelong penalty.

The penalties you're avoiding

Timing isn't just paperwork. Go without Part B when you should have enrolled and Medicare can add 10% to your Part B premium for each full 12-month period you delayed — for life. Go without creditable drug coverage and a Part D late penalty (1% of the national base premium per month) gets added to your drug plan premium for as long as you have Part D. Enrolling on time, even in a low-cost plan, protects you long-term.

Your Salt Lake City checklist

Three months before you turn 65, gather your list of doctors and medications, decide between Original Medicare (often with a Medigap plan and a Part D drug plan) or a Medicare Advantage plan, and enroll. As an independent, Sandy-based agency, we'll walk you through it in plain English and make sure your local doctors and prescriptions are covered before you commit — at no cost to you.

Frequently asked questions

When should I sign up for Medicare if I'm turning 65?

During your Initial Enrollment Period — the 7-month window around your 65th birthday. Enroll in the 3 months before your birthday month so coverage starts on time.

Can I delay Medicare if I'm still working at 65?

Often yes, if you have creditable employer coverage — you may qualify for a Special Enrollment Period later. Confirm your situation first, because delaying incorrectly can cause lifelong penalties.

What happens if I miss my Medicare enrollment window?

You may face a lifelong Part B penalty (10% for each full 12 months delayed) and a Part D late-enrollment penalty, unless you had other creditable coverage.
